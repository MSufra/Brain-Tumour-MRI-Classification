About

No Tumor

These MRIs depict healthy brains with no obvious tumour.

Glioma

A Glioma is a tumour of the glial cells in the brain and spinal cord.
They are the most common type of brain tumor and make up around 80 percent of all malignant tumours.

Meningioma

A Meningioma is a tumour of the meninges, which is the membranous layers surrounding the brain and spinal cord.
Meningiomas are often benign and cases that do result in symptoms can often be resolved via surgery.

Pituitary Tumor

Pituitary adenomas are tumors that occur in the pituitary gland.
Most adenomas are benign but may result in hormane imbalances.
